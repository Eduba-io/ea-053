The opening

What we already see from the outside.

You launched on NSU's campus last fall. 200 users by year-end. Four more South Florida campuses on the map for the next run. Brickell dev shop on iOS. Stripe on payments. Grant money, not venture money. That is a real business in a category where most of the graveyard is thicker than the living.

We are not here to sell you an AI layer. At your stage, most of what matters is below that layer: Android parity, marketplace density per campus, trust-and-safety plumbing, a dispute pipeline that does not route through the CampusRun founders personally.

The frame

How we think about stacks like yours.

Most problems split into three layers. Teams burn budget putting a language model where a well-written Postgres query would do the job faster and cheaper. At CampusRun specifically, dispute resolution, Runner verification, and campus onboarding are almost all the first two layers. The third shows up later. You are not there yet.

60%

Traditional code & database

Payments, escrow, matching feed, Poster and Runner records, Android parity. Postgres, queues, cron. Boring and load-bearing.

30%

Rule-based logic

Dispute states, refund triggers, campus eligibility, Runner verification gates, payout thresholds. Rules you can write down on one page.

10%

Genuine AI work

Fraud-pattern detection at scale. Matching quality at density. Useful later, when the first two layers are solid and you have the volume to train on.

The honest read.

The right move is to build the 60 and 30 properly first. The 10 is a later conversation, after you have Android, liquidity, and a dispute pipeline that runs without you in the middle of it.

The precedent

The closest engagement we have run to yours.

A product-company advisory with a founding CTO. Scoped sprint, workshop, organizational context architecture, a strategic operations framework he could hand to his team. That worked because the CTO had a team. You don't yet. So we would not sell you that.

What we would do

A short advisory session.

Not a consulting engagement. An honest read on where your time is going, what to build versus buy, and which parts of your roadmap belong with our engineering partner rather than with us.

What we would not do

Sell you an orchestration layer.

You need Android parity, payments hardening, and production engineering before you need agent architecture. Putting us on a retainer today would be the wrong call.
